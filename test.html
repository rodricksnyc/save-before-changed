Participant Worksheet

The Participant Worksheet obtains information on the total number and aggregate characteristics of students reached by initiatives designed to broaden participation in STEM. Specifically, the worksheet gathers information on the (1) number of students reached in a given year; (2) gender identity, race, and ethnicity of those students; and (3) number of first-generation college students (i.e., postsecondary students for whom neither parent completed a bachelor’s degree or higher) reached. All of these data are collected in the aggregate, and no information about the characteristics of individual students are collected or displayed as part of this effort.

Several caveats regarding the use of this Participant Worksheet are worth noting:

  • The Participant Worksheet does not collect information about the types or dosage of activities that students participated in. Thus, for example, data cannot be used to distinguish between those students who participated in a 1-hour classroom activity and those who attended a 5-day educational summer program.
  • The data collected through the worksheet represents a duplicated count of students—i.e., the number of times that students were reached (both within and across projects). Thus, for example, a student who participated in two separate activities in a given year would be counted twice.
  • The Participant Worksheet collects data on students reached directly and indirectly. Direct refers to students directly engaged in the STEM broadening participation strategies conducted by a given project (e.g., students who knowingly participated in a defined activity designed to enhance their career awareness about STEM). Indirect refers to those students indirectly engaged by a project (e.g., students who benefited from a defined broadening participation STEM education reform program, event, or activity without being aware of their engagement in that program, event, or activity).
  • For the purposes of the Participant Worksheet, school year is defined as September 1 to August 31. Projects may make changes to this timeline (e.g., including summer activities in the previous school year)—and these changes are documented in a caveats sheet and maintained throughout the duration of the collection.

Participant Worksheet

The Partner Worksheet obtains the following information on each organization that is serving as a partner to an initiative that is designed to broaden participation in STEM: (1) organization name; (2) department (if applicable); (3) organization type; (4) partner role; and (5) partner zip code. Data on partner characteristics represent a cumulative, unduplicated count (although partner organizations are counted multiple times if they are associated with more than one STEM BP initiative).

STEM BP Resources Worksheet

The STEM BP Resources Worksheet obtains the following information on each STEM BP Resource that has been developed by Network members: (1) name of STEM BP resource; (2) resource type; (3) year published or developed; (4) authors (if applicable); (5) publisher (if applicable); (6) description; and (7) link to the resource (if applicable). Data on the characteristics of STEM BP resources represent a cumulative, unduplicated count.
